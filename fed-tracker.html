Cause & Effect

More dollars, chasing the same goods

While some would say a growing economy needs a growing money supply — that as long as money expands roughly in line with output and population it need not be inflationary, and that an active central bank can cushion shocks the gold standard could not — the money supply has not grown "roughly in line" with anything: it leapt ~40% in two pandemic years alone, and sits at a record today even as the Fed claims to be fighting inflation. When new dollars are created faster than real goods, each existing dollar buys less. That is not a side effect; it is the definition. The 1913 Index is simply the receipt.
