Who Paid

Paid for with a printing press

These wars were not paid for out of pocket. They were overwhelmingly debt-financed — borrowed into existence, added to the national debt, and ultimately monetized by the Federal Reserve. That is why "interest on the war debt" is a line item large enough to rival the fighting itself, and why the bill keeps growing for decades after the last shot.

While some would say national defense is the federal government's first duty — that deterrence and alliances have real value, that borrowing to win a war is justified when vital interests are at stake, and that not every dollar here was wasted — borrowing hides the price. A war paid for with taxes is one the public feels and can veto; a war paid for with debt and a printing press is one whose cost is smuggled into the future — and onto every dollar you hold. The erosion the 1913 Index measures and these war bills are the same phenomenon seen from two ends: money created to spend today is purchasing power taken from your savings tomorrow.
