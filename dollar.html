One dollar, 113 years later

Why the two differ

CPI tries to measure the cost of an equivalent standard of living, applying substitution and quality adjustments and a survey-based housing number. The 1913 Index measures the blunt thing: the actual same goods, in actual dollars. Because real prices — above all housing — have outrun CPI, our dollar has lost even more than the government admits.
