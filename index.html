The Reason

Why our number is higher

CPI measures the cost of an equivalent standard of living — so it leans on substitutions, quality adjustments, and a survey-based housing figure. The 1913 Index answers a blunter question: how much more do the actual same goods cost in actual dollars? The single biggest difference is housing — CPI uses "Owners' Equivalent Rent," a survey asking homeowners to guess what their house would rent for, for about a third of the whole index. We use the real price of a home instead.

Nothing Hidden

How we calculate this

1

Real prices, not hypotheticals. Every category mirrors CPI's own basket and weights, but uses real transaction prices — with home prices replacing CPI's imagined "owners' equivalent rent."

2

Every figure sourced and rated. BLS, Census, Shiller, EIA and Treasury data, each with a confidence rating; estimates and pre-1950 figures are flagged.

3

We steelman the other side. Where the official number is actually fair, we say so. That honesty is what makes the rest credible.
