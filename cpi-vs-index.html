Official CPI vs. The 1913 Index

Cumulative inflation since 1913. CPI measures the cost of an equivalent standard of living — hence substitution, hedonic quality adjustments, and a survey-based housing number. The 1913 Index answers the blunter question: how much more do the actual same goods cost in actual dollars.

How the v1 number is built — in the open

The single biggest swap: CPI uses Owners' Equivalent Rent — a survey asking homeowners what they imagine their home would rent for — for about a third of its entire index. We discard that hypothetical and use the real median sale price of a home. That one honest substitution is most of the gap you see above.
