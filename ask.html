Natural-language, no black box

Ask what a dollar used to buy

Type a question about any tracked price or about the dollar itself. Every answer is computed directly from the open, sourced dataset — with its source and confidence rating shown. This engine never invents a number: if it can't ground an answer in the data, it tells you so.

Ask the Index

It understands six kinds of questions: an item over time ("milk in 1960"), the dollar's purchasing power ("$1 in 1913 today"), priced in gold ("a home in gold since 1971"), in hours of work ("a car in hours of work"), compare two items ("milk vs gas"), and leaderboards ("what rose the most since 1971?"). Every answer is computed from the open dataset — browse it on the data page; see how confidence is rated on the method page.

Answers are deterministic — computed from window.DB / window.CORPUS / window.SERIES, every figure sourced. Not investment advice.
